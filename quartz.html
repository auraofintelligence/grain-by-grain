One of the five minerals · back to the sand

Quartz: almost the whole island

Pick up a handful of white Straddie sand. You're holding the same stuff as your kitchen window, your phone screen and the solar panels on someone's roof. It's the plainest thing on the beach and quietly one of the most useful. Here's what it could turn into one day.

The plain stuff

Cleaner sand than almost anywhere.

Known

Most of the island, by weight, is this one pale sand. What makes ours special is how clean it is: barely anything mixed in. That's the whole trick. The cleaner the sand, the less work it takes to turn it into something worth having, which is exactly why it was worth mining here for so long.

What it could turn into

One sand, a whole shelf of things.

Every one of these is something the world already makes from sand, somewhere. The little tags say how close the island could get: some are easy, some are a long climb.

Glass

just melt it

Heat clean sand to around 1,500 degrees, until it runs like honey, then let it cool: you've got glass. Bottles, windows, the sheet a screen starts as. People have done it for thousands of years. All it really takes is a furnace that gets properly hot.

  • Bottles, windows, glassware
    Known

Solar panels and computer chips

the long climb

The shiny heart of a solar panel, and the chip in your phone, both begin as this sand. The catch is the road there: it takes big, power-hungry factories to clean the sand right down and shape it. Solar is a stretch but real. A chip factory is a different planet, billions of dollars and years of work, so that end stays a dream for now.

  • The makings of solar cells
    Modelled
  • Computer chips
    Speculative

Silicone

bendy, water-proof

The soft rubbery stuff in your bakeware, your bathroom sealant, a pair of swimming goggles. Hard to believe, but it starts as sand too.

  • Sealants, bakeware, medical tubing
    Known

A battery made of hot sand

stores warmth for weeks

Here's a lovely one. Pour sand into a big insulated tank, heat it to around 500 to 600 degrees with spare power on a sunny, windy day (hotter than a wood-fired pizza oven), and it holds that warmth for weeks. Pull the heat back out for hot water or drying whenever you need it. There's a real one already running in Finland.

  • A cheap heat store from the cheapest local stuff
    Known

The one catch

The sand is never the hard part.

There's more than enough sand. What every valuable thing above really needs is power to melt and clean it, and people who know how. That's why this sits right next to the island's energy and people plans. Sort those out and the sand is already waiting. Miss them and it stays a lovely beach.
