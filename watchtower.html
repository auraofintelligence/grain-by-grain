The watchtower

Every threat on the previous page gets easier to survive with even a few minutes' notice. Here is the honest state of the art: which warnings genuinely work today, which are real research still being proven, and the "if this, then that" logic that turns a wall of raw data into one plain sentence a person can act on. Watching costs nothing and wastes no one's time; that's why it comes first.

The method

If this, then that.

Known

The logic is deliberately simple: watch a real number, and when it crosses a line, say so in plain words. IF the solar wind speed jumps and the interplanetary field turns south, THEN a geomagnetic storm is likely and it's time to protect sensitive equipment. IF the seafloor shakes and the tide gauges lurch together, THEN move to high ground now, don't wait for a siren. Complexity lives in the sensors; simplicity lives in what the island actually hears.

The data streams

What the watchtower listens to.

Grouped by how far out they're looking, from the sun down to the ground under our feet. Each one carries its honest register.

Solar wind and the magnetic field

the first line of warning

IF solar wind speed climbs past about 500 km/s and the interplanetary magnetic field turns south, THEN a geomagnetic storm is likely within hours, and it's time to protect the grid.

  • Precedent → NOAA and NASA satellites at the L1 point already watch this, continuously, in public data
    KnownThis is the single most mature warning on the whole page, and it's free to plug into. Ties directly to the Carrington card.

Solar flares and eruptions

watching the sun's face directly

IF a strong flare or a coronal mass ejection is seen leaving the sun aimed at Earth, THEN the storm's arrival time and strength can be estimated in advance.

  • Precedent → satellite solar imaging is continuous and public; AI models like Surya (NASA and IBM, open-sourced) are pushing flare-prediction lead times further
    KnownReal, improving every year, and exactly the open tool this island's sovereign nodes could run locally.

Seafloor and tide sensors

the tsunami's first footprint

IF the seafloor shakes with the signature of a big undersea event, and nearby tide gauges lurch in a way normal tides don't, THEN treat it as a tsunami warning immediately, no waiting for confirmation.

  • Precedent → Australia's national tsunami warning system already runs this exact logic
    KnownThe island's own hydrophones (see ocean energy) can extend this network locally, cheaply, as a side benefit of the energy mesh.

Ground swarms and slow tremors

watching the restless calderas

IF a monitored volcanic system shows rising ground, swarms of small quakes, and warming gas emissions together, THEN raise the alert level well before any eruption.

  • Precedent → Campi Flegrei, Yellowstone and Taupo are all instrumented and watched this way today
    KnownDistant from this island physically, but the ash-dimmed-sky risk on the supervolcano card is global, and this is how the world gets its notice.

Radon, water chemistry and animal behaviour

the folk wisdom, tested properly

IF radon gas or groundwater chemistry shifts sharply with no weather explanation, or IF community reports of unusual animal behaviour cluster in one area, THEN treat it as one more vote in the same room, not proof on its own.

  • Status → genuinely researched as earthquake precursors; no single one of these reliably predicts anything alone
    DisputedHonest placement: interesting, worth logging, never the sole trigger for an evacuation. This is exactly the kind of job a shared model does best: weighing many weak signals together instead of letting any one be oversold.

The solar cycle and seismic correlation

the frontier's own live question

IF long-term solar activity and long-term earthquake records are laid side by side, does a pattern actually appear, or is it noise dressed up as signal?

  • Status → a live, unresolved question at the frontier of heliophysics and geophysics
    Disputed SpeculativeThis is precisely the honest, testable form of the micro-nova question: stop arguing about it, run the correlation on real public data, publish the result, and let the numbers speak either way.

Why watching always wins

  • The mature streams (space weather, tsunami, volcanic monitoring) cost nothing extra to plug into: the world already runs them in public.
  • The disputed streams (radon, animal behaviour, solar-seismic correlation) cost almost nothing to log honestly, and every one of them either strengthens the case or quietly falls away as more data comes in.
  • A community that watches openly never has to win an argument to justify watching. That's the whole point of a watchtower: it works whether the frontier turns out right or wrong.
