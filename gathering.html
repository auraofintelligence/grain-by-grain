Places to gather

Dig under the island and the sand comes back up. The same sand could shape seats, circles and shade for the things a community actually does together: a yarn, a market, a festival, a celebration of Country.

The simple idea

The same sand could hold a crowd.

Modelled

None of this is built. It is a set of ideas about turning a pile of dug sand into places to sit, meet and celebrate, instead of carting it away. Each one below is a could, a would, a maybe one day. The bigger the gathering, the more of the dig it takes.

Five places to gather

From a quiet yarn to the whole island.

Five gathering places, smallest first. Each is worked out from the sand a tunnel gives up, so the numbers grow as the crowd does.

Small yarn circle

a quiet place to sit and talk

A ring of seat stones around a centre marker, with footings for a bit of shade over the top. Room for a handful of people to sit close and have a yarn. Small enough for a corner of the park.

  • about 2 tip-truck loads of sand, the spoil from roughly 3 metres of a people-sized tunnel
    Modelled

Community meeting circle

room for the whole group

Curved benches set in arcs around a centre, shade to sit under, and low markers to carry a story or a name. A place for a bigger meeting, where everyone can see each other and be heard.

  • about 5 tip-truck loads of sand, the spoil from roughly 10 metres of a people-sized tunnel
    Modelled

Market-day sitting ring

somewhere to trade and rest

Firm edges to set a stall along, seats scattered for a rest between the aisles, and footings for shade over the busy bits. The bones of a small market the island could put up on a Saturday.

  • about 10 tip-truck loads of sand, the spoil from roughly 21 metres of a people-sized tunnel
    Modelled

Amphitheatre listening slope

seats stepped into the hill

Rows of seating stepped into a natural slope, tidy edges on each step, and a plinth down the front for whoever is speaking or playing. Let the hill do half the work of holding people up, facing a stage.

  • about 29 tip-truck loads of sand, the spoil from roughly 57 metres of a people-sized tunnel
    Modelled

Island festival civic field

open ground for the big days

A wide field for the days the whole island turns out: seating spread across it, shade at the edges, firm ground for a stage, and clear signs to find your way. A place for a festival or a celebration of Country, and anything on or near the shore would need the Quandamooka people's say, through QYAC.

  • about 58 tip-truck loads of sand, the spoil from roughly 115 metres of a people-sized tunnel
    Modelled

Before any of it

Count the sand, and ask first.

You can count the sand a tunnel gives and see how far one dig would stretch across this list. These are places for the island's own good, somewhere to sit, trade and gather, not for export or profit. And anything on or near the shore needs the Quandamooka people's say, through QYAC. Nothing here is presumed.
