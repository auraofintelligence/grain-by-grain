Grain by grain

One ladder from the Gumpi jetty to a civilisation that keeps the lights on through anything. Every step is something a real person can take: a photo taken, a skill learned, a model run, a film made. When cost, law or authority blocks a step, it moves to simulation and cinema, never to the bin. You don't need permission to start, and you don't need money to belong. You need first principles and a place to stand.

First principles, worn openly

Two lanes. Five labels. No waiting.

Every part runs in two lanes, and every claim carries a plain label that just says how sure we are. That way the dreaming can be bold and you always know how solid the ground is. Mainstream and fringe get the same deal: an idea earns a stronger label when someone turns it into a model anyone can run against the same numbers. Nothing rests on who said it; anyone can re-check it for themselves. The full method, five labels and all, is on how to read this site.

First principles include the law

The walls, named.

Four walls stand around this ladder: the mining ban, the nuclear prohibition (a wall that is visibly moving), the water licence, where roughly twenty megalitres a day leave the island's aquifer for the mainland, and zoning that no longer matches the land it describes. Each wall is where a Screen lane begins, and every number carries its sources.
