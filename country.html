The line that comes first

Country

Minjerribah is Quandamooka Country. It was Quandamooka Country before any of this, and it will be long after. This site is not the voice of Quandamooka people, it does not speak for them, and it holds no authority over their land, sea or story. That isn't a disclaimer buried at the bottom of a page. It's the first line, because it comes first.

Say it plainly

What this site is, and what it is not.

Known

A community member can dream out loud about the place they live without pretending to speak for its Traditional Owners. Those are two different things, and this site works hard to never blur them. So, in plain words:

What it is

One resident's open workbench of ideas about the island's long future, published so anyone can read, check, argue with or fork it. Thinking done in the open, on labelled lanes, nothing hidden.

What it is not

Not a Quandamooka project, plan or position. Not an approval, an endorsement or a claim of cultural authority. Not a proposal to dig, mine or build anything on Country without the say-so of the people whose Country it is.

Whose call it is

QYAC is the doorway. Not this website.

Known

Quandamooka people have native title over Minjerribah, recognised in law in 2011. Decisions about the land and the waters go through the Quandamooka Yoolooburrabee Aboriginal Corporation, QYAC, which is the proper doorway for anything real. Not a comment box on a website. Not a resident with a good idea. The doorway.

So every part of this that touches the ground, the tunnels, the reefs, the water, the materials, carries the same rule: it stays a drawing, a model or a film until it goes through that doorway and is welcomed, on Quandamooka terms. The dreaming doesn't need permission. The digging absolutely does, and this site never pretends otherwise.

The measuring stick

Respect isn't a vibe. It has receipts.

Known

It's easy to write "we respect Country" and mean nothing by it. This site tries to mean something by it, by pointing at what real respect has actually looked like when Traditional Owners have held the pen. The benchmark isn't a royalty cheque and a plaque. It's equity, a seat at the table, and the power to say no.

The Mirning people's deal in the Western Green Energy Hub is the standard: real ownership, a permanent board seat, a cultural exclusion zone, and a veto. The Noongar Boodja Trust is the other: a locked fund the community controls, built to outlast the people who signed it. Those are the bar. Any future project on this island that offers less than Traditional Owners have already won elsewhere is offering less than the going rate, and should be told so. The full receipts are on the people page, and the money mechanics on patient capital.

The reason it's built in, not bolted on

The island already paid for the lesson.

This isn't caution for its own sake. Mineral sand mining ran on Minjerribah for around eighty years. The ore went to the mainland and the world. The holes stayed. Whatever that era bought, this Country carried a cost it never chose, and the wariness that leaves behind is earned, not irrational.

So the long-term civilisation planning this site imagines has that lesson built into its foundations, not painted on afterwards. A future of things that last, owned by the people who live with them, held to Country: that only means anything if the people whose Country it is are the ones holding the key. Everything else on this ladder is downstream of that.

Grain by grain, rung by rung. Not an official project page: a public spine for a family of open workbenches.
